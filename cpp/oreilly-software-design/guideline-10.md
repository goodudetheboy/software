# Guideline 10: Consider Creating an Architectural Document

One interesting quote I see in here

> In most successful software projects, the expert developers working on that project have a shared understanding of the system design. This shared understanding is called "architecture".

I must say, this is a pretty cool understanding of software architecture. This emphasizes how important communication is when working software.

I think the author base on this point to show that it's important to have one concrete physical place where people can align their vision of the piece of software the team is working on, since people come from different backgrounds and experiences. As the author says, this will allow the team to move your software in the same direction, or at least help.

Document also preserves ideas, visions, and decisions. Helps prevent loss of "tribal knowledge" once the main software architect leaves the team. This makes code essentially "legacy".

Also one cool thing I learn: rewrites are "the single worst strategic mistake that any company can make".

Cool, there are tools that can help you statically enforce the architecture of your codebase (Axivion Suite, Sparx Systems Enteprise Architect)
